Masahiro Hara
Masahiro Hara (Japanese: 原 昌宏, Hepburn: Hara Masahiro, born 1957) is a Japanese engineer and Hosei University graduate who is best known for inventing the QR code in 1994.

The Birth of QR Codes

QR codes, or Quick Response codes, were invented in 1994 by Denso Wave, a subsidiary of Toyota, to track vehicles during the manufacturing process. They were designed to allow for quick decoding of information.

How QR Codes Work

QR codes store data using patterns of black dots and squares arranged on a grid. This data can be read by scanning the code with a QR code reader, typically a smartphone. The code can store various types of information, such as URLs, text, or other data.

Why QR Codes Were Created

The primary reason for creating QR codes was to enhance the capability of barcode technology. QR codes can store more data and can be scanned quickly from any direction, making them highly efficient for tracking and information retrieval.

Platforms Using QR Codes

QR codes are used across various platforms including:

  • Payments and Banking (e.g., PayPal, Venmo)
  • Social Media (e.g., Facebook, Instagram)
  • Marketing and Advertising
  • Product Packaging
  • Event Management

The Father of QR Codes

The father of QR codes is Masahiro Hara, an engineer at Denso Wave. He led the team that developed the technology in the early 1990s.
